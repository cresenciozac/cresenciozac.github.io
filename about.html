About Me

I'm a Filipino-American Electrical Engineering student at the University of Minnesota, interested in the way energy is generated and power can be moved. I think the large-scale focus of Electrical Engineering is the most interesting part, and I hope that in the future I can work on researching, developing, and implementing sustainable energy and power systems.

I've worked as an intern at MEYER BORGMAN JOHNSON in Minneapolis, and have participated in the Solar Vehicle Project array team at the University of Minnesota, and both of those experiences have been pretty fun! For more information about those, feel free to check out my resume.
As for my personal life, I enjoy a variety of random hobbies. I've played the cello for 10 years, and that's always been a huge part of my life. I love learning new music and playing with an ensemble. I'd love to learn every instrument under the sun, but it's definitely an expensive hobby! I also make my own songs on the side— I think it's enjoyable and helps get that creative energy flowing.

I've also been exploring longboarding (see picture), and by no means am I any good, but it's healing and good fun. Some more random things I enjoy are the mechas from Neon Genesis Evangelion, fountain pens, listening to jazz, and Minecraft. Honestly, I love discovering new things and trying things out, so don't take these as a definitive list!

Lastly, I will never say no to some good food. Enough said.
